SECTION 00
Overview — MSA in U.S. public procurement
WHAT THIS TRACKS

MSA Safety Incorporated (NYSE: MSA, formerly Mine Safety Appliances) sells life-safety equipment that is largely required by regulators and bought by government end-users — fire departments, the military, and federal agencies. This page tracks every federal prime contract award to MSA's entities with contract activity in the window, sourced directly from the U.S. government's spending database.

It exists to put hard, public numbers behind the thesis claims: a fire-services franchise riding an SCBA replacement cycle, a growing detection book, and recurring, non-cyclical government demand.

SECTION 06
Methodology & sources
Full traceability — every number here regenerates from public APIs.
HOW THIS WAS BUILT

Source. U.S. Treasury USAspending.gov API (the authoritative federal procurement record, fed by FPDS). We query all prime contract awards (award types A/B/C/D) for MSA's recipient entities with contract activity in the window.

Entities.

Segment mapping. Each award is classified into an MSA segment — Fire Services (SCBA), Detection, or Industrial PPE — using a priority of product description keywords → PSC product code → NAICS industry code. Awards that don't cleanly map (e.g. generic support services, maintenance kits) are left as Other / Unclassified rather than force-fit.

Scope & caveats. Federal prime contracts only. This excludes state & local tenders (fragmented, no single API) and federal sub-awards where MSA sells through a distributor — so true government demand for MSA product is larger than shown. "Year" is the award action's period-of-performance start date. A small number of awards have a start date before the 5-year window but remained active within it.

Reproducibility. Re-run scripts/collect_federal.py then scripts/build_analysis.py to refresh every figure on this page.
