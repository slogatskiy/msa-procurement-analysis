SECTION 00
Overview — MSA in U.S. public procurement
WHAT THIS TRACKS

MSA Safety Incorporated (NYSE: MSA, formerly Mine Safety Appliances) sells life-safety equipment that is largely required by regulators and bought by government end-users — fire departments, the military, and federal agencies. This page tracks every federal prime contract award to MSA's entities with contract activity in the window, sourced directly from the U.S. government's spending database.

It exists to put hard, public numbers behind the thesis claims: a fire-services franchise riding an SCBA replacement cycle, a growing detection book, and recurring, non-cyclical government demand.

WHAT THIS MEANS — AND THE LIMIT ON "LOST" BIDS

On bids MSA lost: U.S. federal databases publish winners, not the full bidder list, so a clean "MSA bid and lost" federal tally does not exist publicly. The honest proxies are here: the open-competition wins above (MSA beating rivals head-to-head) and the bid-count chart (how many competitors showed up). Where MSA loses is most visible in state/local bid tabulations — a future add. See also the GAO bid-protest note in Methodology.

ON RECORD — GAO BID PROTESTS (HEAD-TO-HEAD WITH RIVALS)

MSA won & defended it (2021): Scott Technologies (3M's Scott Fire & Safety, MSA's #1 SCBA rival) protested the U.S. Air Force's award to MSA Safety Sales, LLC for commercial SCBA airpaks, masks and respirator kits (RFP FA8534-20-R-0006). GAO denied the protest — MSA's win stood against its biggest competitor. [GAO B-421025.2]

MSA lost & challenged it (1992): Mine Safety Appliances protested a U.S. Navy award to National Draeger for a shipboard Fire Fighters Breathing Apparatus. GAO denied MSA's protest — a documented case of MSA competing and not winning. [GAO B-242379.5]

Source: U.S. Government Accountability Office bid-protest decisions (gao.gov/legal/bid-protests). Illustrative, hand-verified cases — not an exhaustive list.

SECTION 06
FEMA AFG — the demand engine behind SCBA
Assistance to Firefighters Grants: federal money paid straight to fire departments to buy equipment — prominently SCBA. A leading indicator of MSA fire-services demand.
WHY THIS MATTERS

Fire departments rarely pay for new breathing apparatus out of pocket — they fund it with AFG grants. Regulations bar running SCBA more than two NFPA generations old, and grant money can't be spent on it either, so each new standard cycle pushes departments to re-buy. MSA doesn't receive these grants (departments do), but this is the federal fuel that gets spent on MSA G1 SCBA. Tracking national AFG volume is a clean, public read on the demand pool.

SECTION 09
Methodology & sources
Full traceability — every number here regenerates from public APIs.
HOW THIS WAS BUILT

Source. U.S. Treasury USAspending.gov API (the authoritative federal procurement record, fed by FPDS). We query all prime contract awards (award types A/B/C/D) for MSA's recipient entities with contract activity in the window.

Entities.

Segment mapping. Each award is classified into an MSA segment — Fire Services (SCBA), Detection, or Industrial PPE — using a priority of product description keywords → PSC product code → NAICS industry code. Awards that don't cleanly map (e.g. generic support services, maintenance kits) are left as Other / Unclassified rather than force-fit.

State & local. There is no single API for the 50 states and thousands of cities, so we run an automated sweep of government open-data "checkbook" portals on the Socrata platform: discover vendor-payment datasets across all domains, auto-detect the vendor/amount columns, and filter for payments booked to MSA's legal entities. Look-alike vendors (e.g. "Missouri Mine Safety & Health") and non-payment records are filtered out.

FEMA AFG. Pulled from USAspending assistance awards under Assistance Listing 97.044. AFG is paid to fire departments, not MSA — we use national volume as a demand proxy.

Bid protests. GAO decisions are read by hand (no bulk API); the cases shown are verified and illustrative, not exhaustive.

Scope & caveats. Federal numbers are prime contracts only; sub-awards through distributors are excluded. State/local capture is limited to Socrata-published jurisdictions and payments booked directly to MSA (reseller sales are invisible). So true government demand for MSA is larger than shown everywhere on this page. "Year" is the award action's period-of-performance start date; a few federal awards started before the window but stayed active in it.

Reproducibility. Re-run scripts/collect_federal.py, scripts/collect_socrata.py, scripts/collect_afg.py, then scripts/build_analysis.py to refresh every figure on this page.
